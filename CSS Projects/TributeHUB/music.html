About

Ludwig van Beethoven was a German composer and pianist who bridged the Classical and Romantic periods of Western music. Born in Bonn in 1770, Beethoven demonstrated remarkable musical talent from childhood and later established himself as one of Europe's most celebrated composers. Despite gradually losing his hearing during adulthood, Beethoven continued to compose some of the most powerful and emotionally profound works in musical history. His perseverance in the face of adversity remains one of the most inspiring stories in the arts. His symphonies, sonatas, and concertos transformed musical expression, emphasizing individuality, passion, and human emotion. Today, Beethoven's music continues to resonate with audiences across cultures and generations.
