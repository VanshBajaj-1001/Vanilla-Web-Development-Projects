Albert Einstein

1879-1955

"Imagination is more important than knowledge."

About

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist whose revolutionary ideas transformed humanity's understanding of the universe. Born in Ulm, Germany, in 1879, Einstein displayed an extraordinary curiosity about nature from an early age. Although he struggled with traditional education systems, his passion for mathematics and physics eventually led him to become one of history's greatest scientific minds. In 1905, often called his "miracle year," Einstein published four groundbreaking papers that changed modern physics forever. His work on special relativity introduced the famous equation E = mc², establishing the relationship between mass and energy. Later, his theory of general relativity redefined gravity as the curvature of space-time, fundamentally altering our perception of the cosmos. Beyond science, Einstein advocated for peace, human rights, and international cooperation. His intellectual legacy extends far beyond laboratories, symbolizing creativity, curiosity, and the relentless pursuit of knowledge.

Contributions

  • Theory of Special Relativity (1905)
  • Theory of General Relativity (1915)
  • Explanation of the photoelectric effect
  • Contributions to quantum theory

Legacy

Einstein remains a symbol of curiosity, creativity, and scientific genius worldwide.
