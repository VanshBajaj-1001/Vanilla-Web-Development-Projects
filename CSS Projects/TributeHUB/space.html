About

Neil Armstrong was an American astronaut, naval aviator, and aerospace engineer best known as the first human being to walk on the Moon. Born in Ohio in 1930, Armstrong developed a fascination with aviation early in life and earned his pilot's license before obtaining a driver's license. After serving as a naval pilot, he joined NASA and participated in several pioneering space missions. His calm demeanor, technical expertise, and leadership made him a trusted figure during one of humanity's greatest scientific endeavors. On July 20, 1969, Armstrong stepped onto the lunar surface during the Apollo 11 mission, uttering the historic words, "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." His achievement became a defining moment in human history and symbolized humanity's spirit of exploration.
