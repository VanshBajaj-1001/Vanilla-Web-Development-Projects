A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

About

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist, educator, and statesman who played a central role in India's technological and defense advancements. Born in Rameswaram in 1931, Kalam overcame humble beginnings through determination, hard work, and an unwavering commitment to education. His contributions to India's missile and space programs earned him the title "Missile Man of India." Working with organizations such as ISRO and DRDO, he helped strengthen India's capabilities in aerospace engineering and national defense. Later, as the President of India, Dr. Kalam became known as the "People's President." His emphasis on youth empowerment, scientific thinking, and dreaming big continues to inspire millions of students and innovators across the world

Contributions

  • Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme
  • Contributions to ISRO and DRDO
  • Author and educator
