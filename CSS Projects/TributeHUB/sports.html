About

Muhammad Ali was an American boxer, humanitarian, and activist whose influence reached far beyond the world of sports. Born Cassius Clay in 1942, he displayed extraordinary athletic talent from a young age and quickly rose to prominence as one of boxing's greatest champions. Known for his charisma, confidence, and unmatched skill, Ali became a three-time heavyweight world champion. His famous declaration, "I am the greatest," reflected both his self-belief and his revolutionary impact on professional athletics. Outside the ring, Ali courageously advocated for civil rights and religious freedom. His refusal to participate in the Vietnam War made him a global symbol of conviction and moral courage, ensuring that his legacy extends far beyond sporting achievements.
